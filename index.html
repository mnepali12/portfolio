PORTFOLIO

A Geographic Information System (GIS) portfolio is a collection of projects and maps that demonstrate a person's skills in spatial analysis, cartography, and geospatial data management. It highlights the ability to use GIS tools such as ArcGIS and QGIS to collect, analyze, and visualize geographic data.

A strong GIS portfolio usually includes thematic maps, spatial analysis results, remote sensing outputs, and short explanations of each project's objectives, data sources, and methodology. By presenting well-designed maps and clear descriptions, a GIS portfolio helps showcase technical abilities, problem-solving skills, and practical experience in the field of Geographic Information Systems. It is an important tool for students and professionals when applying for internships, research opportunities, or jobs in geospatial and environmental fields.
